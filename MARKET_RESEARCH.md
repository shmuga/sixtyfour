## 1. Market Demand & Opportunity

### 1.1 The Chess Market is Massive and Growing

- **Chess.com** crossed **200 million registered users** in April 2025, doubling in just over two years
- **11 million daily active users** with **20 million games played daily**
- The global chess market reached **$3.45 billion in 2025**, projected to hit **$7.66 billion by 2034**
- FIDE estimates **605 million people** play chess regularly worldwide
- Chess.com ranks in the **top 90 websites globally** and continues growing (traffic up 2.61% month-over-month as of Jan 2026)
- Demographics: **65.1% male, 34.9% female**, largest age group **25–34 year olds** — prime smartphone/widget users

### 1.2 Unmet Demand for Chess.com Companion Widgets

There is **clear, documented demand** for exactly what SixtyFour does:

- **Chess.com forum users have actively requested** widget support for ratings, daily performance, streaks, and accuracy stats — Chess.com has **not built this** into their official app
- The only existing competitor, **Elo Widgets** (by Richard Billmeier), provides basic rating tracking on the home screen but **lacks goal tracking, puzzle progress, analytics, notifications, or activity rings**
- **Chess Widget** (by Oleg Soloviev) is a different product entirely — it shows puzzles on the widget to solve, not progress tracking
- No app currently offers **daily puzzle/game goal tracking with widgets** — SixtyFour fills a genuine gap

### 1.3 Competitive Landscape

| App | Focus | Widgets | Goal Tracking | Analytics | Price |
|-----|-------|---------|---------------|-----------|-------|
| **SixtyFour** | Puzzle & game progress tracking | Small + Medium (activity rings) | Daily targets with smart notifications | 30-day history with accuracy/win rate | TBD |
| **Elo Widgets** | Rating display | Yes (basic) | No | No | Free |
| **Chess Widget** | Puzzle solving on widget | Yes (puzzle board) | No | No | Free |
| **Chess.com App** | Full chess platform | No widgets | No daily goals | Basic stats in-app | Freemium ($6.99–$13.99/mo) |

**Key insight:** SixtyFour is the **only app** combining Chess.com progress tracking + home screen widgets + daily goal system + historical analytics. This is a unique value proposition with no direct competitor.

### 1.4 Demand Signals

- 70% of App Store visitors discover apps via **search** — chess-related keywords have consistent high volume
- Chess.com users are **highly engaged** (daily habit-forming activity) and actively seek tools to track improvement
- The "Duolingo streak" mechanic is proven to drive retention — SixtyFour applies this to chess
- Widget apps consistently perform well because they deliver **passive, glanceable value**

## 2. Pricing Strategy

### 2.1 Recommended Price: **$2.99 (one-time purchase)**

#### Why $2.99?

| Factor | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| **Value positioning** | Premium enough to signal quality, affordable enough for impulse purchase |
| **Market benchmarks** | Launcher Pro ($2.99), Widget Wizard Pro ($3.99), Chess-Studio ($3.99), Tydlig ($1.99) — $2.99 sits in the sweet spot |
| **No subscription fatigue** | Chess.com already charges $6.99–$13.99/mo — users appreciate a **one-time purchase** alternative. Users explicitly praise apps like Things 3 for avoiding subscriptions |
| **Target audience** | Chess.com users already pay for the platform; a $2.99 companion tool is a trivial add-on |
| **Conversion psychology** | $2.99 is below the psychological $3 threshold while still above $1.99 (which can signal low quality) |

#### Alternative Models Considered

| Model | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| **Free** | Not recommended — no revenue, harder to sustain development, attracts low-engagement users |
| **$0.99** | Too low — signals throwaway quality, poor revenue per download |
| **$1.99** | Viable but leaves money on the table — the feature set justifies $2.99 |
| **$3.99** | Slightly risky for a companion/utility app — better reserved if you add premium features later |
| **$4.99+** | Too high for a single-purpose widget/tracker app |
| **Freemium** | Possible future path: free with basic widget, $2.99 to unlock medium widget + analytics + notifications |
| **Subscription** | Not recommended — the app doesn't provide enough ongoing content to justify recurring payments, and users are fatigued by subscriptions |

#### Revenue Projections (Conservative)

| Scenario | Monthly Downloads | Monthly Revenue | Annual Revenue |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Low | 200 | $420 | $5,040 |
| Medium | 1,000 | $2,100 | $25,200 |
| High | 5,000 | $10,500 | $126,000 |

*Based on $2.99 price after Apple's 30% cut = $2.09 net per download*

## 3. App Store Marketing Materials

### 3.1 App Name (30 characters max)

```
SixtyFour: Chess Goal Tracker
```

**Rationale:** Leads with the brand name, includes "Chess" for search indexing, and "Goal Tracker" communicates the core value. 30 characters exactly.

### 3.2 Subtitle (30 characters max)

```
Puzzles & Ratings Widget
```

**Rationale:** Adds "Puzzles," "Ratings," and "Widget" — three high-value keywords not in the title. 27 characters.

### 3.3 Keywords Field (100 characters max)

```
chess.com,daily,puzzles,tactics,rating,progress,streak,blitz,rapid,bullet,stats,goals,improvement
```

**Rationale:**
- **No duplicates** with title/subtitle (Apple indexes those separately)
- **chess.com** — direct audience targeting (users searching for companion tools)
- **daily, streak, goals** — captures habit/goal-tracking intent
- **puzzles, tactics** — high-volume chess search terms
- **rating, progress, improvement** — captures users seeking growth tools
- **blitz, rapid, bullet** — time class keywords that dedicated players search
- **stats** — utility/analytics intent
- 97 characters used (under 100 limit)

### 3.4 Description

```
Track your Chess.com puzzle and game progress with beautiful home screen widgets.

SixtyFour connects to your Chess.com account and gives you a clear, visual dashboard of your daily chess activity — no password needed.

SET DAILY GOALS

BEAUTIFUL HOME SCREEN WIDGETS

- Daily goal reminders that respect your progress
- Dark theme with amber chess aesthetics
- Pull-to-refresh for instant updates

SixtyFour uses Chess.com's public API. It is not affiliated with or endorsed by Chess.com.
```

## 4. Summary & Recommendations

### Go-to-Market Checklist

1. **Price at $2.99** one-time purchase — strong value positioning with no subscription fatigue
2. **Use the ASO metadata above** — optimized for discovery with zero keyword waste
3. **Lead with widgets in screenshots** — this is the primary differentiator
4. **Target the "chess improvement" niche** — users actively seeking tools to get better
5. **Leverage the "no subscription" angle** in promotional text — this resonates strongly
6. **Submit to App Store under Utilities** with Sports as secondary category
7. **Prepare 5–6 screenshots** following the strategy above
8. **Consider a free tier later** if you want to maximize downloads, with a $2.99 unlock for widgets + analytics

- **200M Chess.com users** and zero apps doing exactly what SixtyFour does
- **Proven widget demand** — community has asked Chess.com for this, and they haven't built it
- **Habit-forming mechanics** — daily goals + streaks + reminders drive retention
- **Premium design** — activity rings and dark theme set it apart from basic competitors
- **No authentication barrier** — works with just a username via public API
- **One-time $2.99** — in a sea of subscriptions, this is refreshing and shareable
